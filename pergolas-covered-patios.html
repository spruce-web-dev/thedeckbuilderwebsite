Pergolas vs Covered Patios

Both pergolas and covered patios create comfortable outdoor living spaces, but they serve slightly different purposes depending on the level of shade and protection you want.

Pergolas

Pergolas feature open slats across the roof structure that allow sunlight to filter through while still providing partial shade. They’re ideal for homeowners who want a lighter architectural feature that enhances the look of their deck or patio.

Covered Patios

Covered patios include a solid roof system that blocks rain, snow, and direct sun. This creates a true outdoor room where you can place furniture, TVs, heaters, and lighting for year-round use.

Custom Hybrid Designs

Many homeowners choose a combination design — for example a pergola structure with polycarbonate roofing panels or adjustable louvers that allow you to control shade throughout the day.

Materials We Use

Colorado’s intense sun, temperature swings, and winter storms require materials that are built to last. We carefully select lumber and structural components that hold up in high-altitude environments.

  • Cedar — naturally rot resistant with a warm, classic look
  • Pressure-treated framing — engineered for structural durability
  • Composite trim — low maintenance with long-term color stability
  • Powder-coated aluminum — modern option with minimal upkeep
  • Polycarbonate roofing panels — allow light through while blocking UV

Design Features & Add-Ons

A pergola or patio cover becomes even more functional when it’s integrated with lighting, heating, and outdoor living features. Many homeowners use these structures as the centerpiece of their backyard entertainment space.

  • Recessed LED lighting for evening gatherings
  • Ceiling fans for airflow during hot summer days
  • Integrated heaters for cool Colorado evenings
  • Outdoor TVs or speaker wiring
  • Privacy screens or slatted wall panels
  • Outdoor kitchen or grilling area integration
